abstract: The contribution of this study is to propose an enhanced Rousselier model by combining Tresca yield criterion to accurately predict both flat and shear-lip fractures. Flat fracture caused by the void growth under high stress triaxiality is realized by introducing void volume fraction as a damage variable that causes the shrinkage of the yield surface associated with the hydrostatic stress. In contrast, shear-lip fracture, which is caused by the void rotation and expansion in shear bands under low stress triaxiality, is represented by incorporating Tresca yield criterion as the dependence on Lode parameter. Throughout several numerical examples, the capability of the proposed model is demonstrated by comparing with the conventional Rousselier model in which von Mises yield criterion is employed as the yield function.
